A Bulleit-proof guide to whiskey

A compilation and editorial by Prashant Singh

What's whiskey?
  • distilled alcohol (liquor)
  • made from fermented grain mash
  • often aged in oak casks
How do you make it?

start with something that has sugar

ferment to get alcohol from sugar

distill to concentrate the alcohol and deliciousness

Why wait years to drink it?

Charred oak barrels act like a filter and refine the whiskey

The wood lends extra flavor

Tip

brown liquor implies aging in wooden casks

What is proof?

Proof is just double the alcohol %

You mostly see proof used at 40% or above (i.e. hard liquor)

35%that's not whiskey, buddy
40%80 proof
45%90 proof. You'll probably notice
50%100 proof. Strong bottling means it should stand up well to a splash of water or some ice
60%120 proof. Topical antiseptic
95.6%Ethanol will pull water from the air, preventing distillation above this strength
10 year, 12 year, 15?

Most nice whiskeys stay in the cask at least 10-12 years

Blended whiskey: a variety of ages combined to produce a consistent product

12 year: the whole thing has been aged 12 years

Older → more expensive

Better depends on what you want

Aging generally smooths out the whiskey, adds more of the barrel's flavor

Neat? On the rocks?
neat/straight-up Just whiskey in a glass. Right on.

on the rocks with ice
